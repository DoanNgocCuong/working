# FPT PLAY SPORTS PAGE REDESIGN
## Product Proposal: From Content Catalog to Daily Destination
### 6-Month Strategic Initiative (December 2025 - May 2026)

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

FPT Play's Sports Page currently serves as a **content catalog** rather than a **daily destination**. While the platform attracts significant traffic during major matches (300% spike during tournaments like AFF Cup), it suffers from critical engagement and retention challenges: only 23% of users return within 7 days (vs. 45% industry average), 68% exhibit single-session behavior ("Open → Find match → Watch → Exit"), and post-match content engagement remains severely underutilized at just 8% highlight viewership.

This proposal outlines a strategic redesign leveraging **AI-powered personalization and habit-forming mechanics** to transform the Sports Page into a destination users visit daily, regardless of match schedules. The initiative targets three core metrics: increasing 7-day retention from 23% to 50%, extending session duration from 8 to 25-30 minutes, and improving conversion rate from 12% to 17%+, ultimately driving ARPU from $36 to $120+ per user.

---

## PART A: MARKET ANALYSIS & USER SEGMENTATION

### A1. MARKET CONTEXT: VIETNAM'S OTT & SPORTS STREAMING BOOM

Vietnam's OTT market is experiencing rapid transformation. The market grew from 5.56 million subscribers in 2023 to approximately 7.4 million in 2024 (33% growth), with total OTT revenue reaching nearly 1.7 trillion VND. This expansion reflects broader global trends: by 2025, streaming services are projected to account for one-fifth of global sports rights spending, exceeding $12 billion annually. In the United States alone, the number of viewers streaming sports at least once monthly is projected to reach 90 million by 2025, up from 57 million in 2021.

Within Vietnam, the sports streaming segment represents a particularly high-growth opportunity. While traditional television still captures 27% of weekly sports viewership, online streaming has reached 23% and is rapidly closing the gap. The Vietnamese demographic consuming sports content skews young and digitally native: 65% of FPT Play's sports audience accesses content via mobile devices, with peak traffic concentrated between 19:00-22:00 (70% of daily traffic). The audience is predominantly male (68%) and concentrated in the 25-34 age group (45% of users), though younger segments (18-24) represent 25% of the user base.

**Key Market Insight:** Southeast Asia's sports tech ecosystem is experiencing a boom characterized by gamification, AI-powered personalization, and mobile-first platforms that transform passive viewers into active participants. Fans in this region demonstrate high willingness to pay for tailored, interactive experiences—a trend validated by the success of platforms like ON Plus (15 million users) and the expansion of betting-integrated sports platforms offering real-time engagement features.

### A2. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE & FPT PLAY'S POSITIONING

FPT Play operates in a highly competitive market with over 35 OTT platforms competing for share. The direct competitors in sports streaming present distinct competitive advantages and weaknesses:

**K+ (Giang)** holds a formidable position through exclusive Premier League rights and V.League broadcasting, delivering 4K quality with sophisticated post-match features including curated highlights, behind-the-scenes content, and tactical analysis. K+ maintains 40-50% 7-day retention and ARPU of 159k-235k VND, though the platform faces closure in December 2025, creating a market opportunity for FPT Play to capture its user base.

**VieON** leverages bundled IPTV distribution with HBO and shared V.League rights, achieving lower ARPU (30k-50k VND) but benefiting from bundled subscription models. The platform lacks post-match content development, representing a weakness FPT Play can exploit.

**VTV Go** provides free V.League streaming with broadcast-quality video, capturing price-sensitive users but offering minimal post-match engagement features.

**Galaxy Play** operates at the low end of the market with basic sports content and ARPU of 10k-20k VND, not directly competing with FPT Play's premium positioning.

Indirect competitors present equally significant threats. **YouTube** generates 20-30 minute average sessions through best-in-class auto-play algorithms and trending feeds, capturing highlight and analysis content that could otherwise remain within FPT Play. **Facebook Groups** foster real-time reactions and community discussion (10-20 minute sessions), while **TikTok** drives 15-25 minute sessions through viral short-form content. **SofaScore** and similar stats platforms provide specialized analytics that some users access via second screens during matches.

**FPT Play's Competitive Advantages:**
- Diverse content portfolio: V.League, European Cup competitions (C1/C2/C3), NBA, Golf, and other sports
- Robust infrastructure: FPT's telecom backbone ensures low-latency streaming, high reliability, and seamless cross-device experience
- Localized identity: In-house commentary team with distinctive, culturally resonant style that differentiates from international or traditional broadcast approaches
- All-in-one ecosystem: Integration of live streaming, highlights, news, and analysis within a single platform

**FPT Play's Current Weaknesses:**
- Post-match content underutilization: Only 8% of users view highlights after matches conclude
- Lack of personalization: All users encounter identical homepage layouts regardless of preferences
- Search functionality limitations: Search recommendations include non-sports content (movies, shows), creating friction
- No community features: Users must exit the app to engage with fan communities
- Information overload: Undifferentiated content presentation causes decision paralysis

### A3. USER SEGMENTATION & PERSONAS

Based on behavioral data and demographic characteristics, FPT Play's sports audience segments into four distinct user types, each with unique motivations, pain points, and revenue potential:

#### **Segment 1: Single-Session Viewers (The Casual Opportunist)**
**Behavioral Profile:** This segment represents the largest user cohort, accounting for 68% of observed user behavior patterns. These users exhibit the classic "Open → Find match → Watch → Exit" pattern, visiting the app exclusively when a specific match aligns with their immediate viewing intent. They spend only 38 seconds on the homepage before navigating to video content and demonstrate minimal engagement with non-live material.

**Demographics:** Predominantly casual sports fans across all age groups, though concentrated in the 18-24 segment (25% of total users). These users view sports as entertainment rather than passion, making them susceptible to distraction from competing platforms like TikTok and YouTube.

**Pain Points:** Difficulty locating specific matches due to poor information architecture; lack of motivation to explore beyond the immediate match; easy distraction by alternative entertainment platforms; no reason to maintain app engagement between matches.

**Retention Metrics:** 7-day retention of 23% (vs. 45% industry average); average session duration of 8 minutes outside of live matches; conversion rate of 12% from sports page to paid subscription.

**Revenue Potential:** Moderate. While this segment represents high volume, individual lifetime value is constrained by low engagement and high churn. However, converting even a portion of this segment to higher-engagement tiers could yield significant revenue uplift.

#### **Segment 2: Core Enthusiasts (The Dedicated Fan)**
**Behavioral Profile:** This segment represents approximately 20-25% of the user base but accounts for disproportionately high engagement and revenue contribution. These users spend 45 minutes per session during active seasons, actively seek post-match analysis and highlights, and demonstrate 2.5x higher retention compared to casual users. They view sports as integral to their identity and emotional experience.

**Demographics:** Concentrated in the 25-34 age group (45% of users), predominantly male (68%), with higher likelihood of paid subscription adoption (30% of user base are paid subscribers). This segment shows strong correlation with premium tier conversion.

**Pain Points:** Difficulty discovering post-match content; lack of community spaces for discussion and analysis; insufficient tactical depth in available analysis; fragmented experience requiring multiple apps (app + Facebook + YouTube + SofaScore) to satisfy information needs; absence of team-specific personalization.

**Retention Metrics:** 7-day retention of 45-50% (approaching industry average); average session duration of 45 minutes during season; conversion rate of 20%+ to paid tiers; CLV of $60-80 per user (vs. $36 platform average).

**Revenue Potential:** High. This segment drives 40% of sports-related revenue despite representing only 20-25% of users. Premium feature adoption (tactical analysis, advanced stats, exclusive content) yields strong monetization potential.

#### **Segment 3: Niche Sport Enthusiasts (The Multi-Sport Explorer)**
**Behavioral Profile:** Users focused on sports beyond football (badminton 35% of users, tennis 18%, basketball 12%, other sports 8%). These users experience significant friction due to information overload—they must scroll through predominantly football-focused content to locate their sports of interest. Current platform design treats all sports equally, failing to recognize the distinct preferences within this segment.

**Demographics:** Distributed across age groups but with notable concentration in 35-45 segment (20% of users). Includes both casual explorers and dedicated enthusiasts within specific sports.

**Pain Points:** Information overload from football-dominant content; lack of personalized filtering by sport; difficulty discovering niche sport content; no community spaces for niche sport fans; poor search functionality for specific sports.

**Retention Metrics:** 7-day retention of 18-22% (below average); session duration of 6-8 minutes (below platform average); conversion rate of 8-10% (below average).

**Revenue Potential:** Moderate to high. While this segment currently underperforms, proper personalization and content curation could unlock significant value. Niche sports fans often demonstrate higher engagement and willingness to pay for specialized content.

#### **Segment 4: Curious Samplers (The Discovery-Oriented User)**
**Behavioral Profile:** Users who browse the homepage frequently, exploring various sports and content types without deep commitment to any single sport or team. They exhibit trend-seeking behavior, viewing sports consumption as part of broader entertainment consumption. They appreciate short-form content and curated recommendations but struggle with decision-making when faced with excessive options.

**Demographics:** Skew younger (18-24, 25% of users), with higher representation of female users (32% of platform) compared to other segments. Often access content via social media discovery rather than direct app visits.

**Pain Points:** Overwhelming content volume without clear guidance on what to watch; lack of short-form content for quick consumption; no "Discover" section comparable to Spotify or YouTube; insufficient trending/trending indicators to guide exploration; poor content recommendation system.

**Retention Metrics:** 7-day retention of 20-25%; session duration of 8-12 minutes; conversion rate of 10%; high sensitivity to content presentation and UX friction.

**Revenue Potential:** Moderate. This segment demonstrates conversion potential if friction is reduced and content discovery improved. However, lower lifetime value due to reduced engagement depth.

### A4. DETAILED PERSONAS

**Persona 1: "Minh" - The Loyal Specialist (Core Enthusiast)**

Minh is a 28-year-old marketing professional and devoted Liverpool FC supporter. He watches nearly every Liverpool match, spending 45+ minutes per session during the season. Minh follows Liverpool across multiple platforms: he watches matches on FPT Play, checks SofaScore for advanced statistics, participates in Liverpool fan groups on Facebook, and browses tactical analysis on YouTube. He's frustrated that FPT Play doesn't provide the depth of tactical analysis he craves, forcing him to leave the app to satisfy his analytical interests.

Minh's key pain points include: (1) inability to find Liverpool-specific content quickly; (2) lack of advanced statistics (xG, possession %, heatmaps) within the app; (3) no community space for discussing tactics and predictions; (4) insufficient post-match analysis content. Despite these frustrations, Minh maintains a paid subscription because FPT Play holds exclusive V.League and European Cup rights he values. However, he's vulnerable to churn if competitors improve their analytical offerings.

**Desired Solution:** A "My Team Universe" feature that personalizes the entire app around Liverpool, combined with tactical analysis tools and a community discussion space where he can engage with fellow fans.

**Persona 2: "Anh Tú" - The Casual Opportunist (Single-Session Viewer)**

Anh Tú is a 22-year-old university student who watches football casually, typically when friends are gathering or when Vietnam plays in major tournaments. He uses FPT Play sporadically—sometimes weeks pass without opening the app. When he does open it, he's looking for one specific match and leaves immediately after watching. Anh Tú finds the current interface confusing; he often uses Google to find match times rather than navigating FPT Play's homepage. He's never paid for a subscription and has minimal brand loyalty.

Anh Tú's key pain points include: (1) difficulty finding specific matches; (2) no compelling reason to explore beyond the immediate match; (3) distraction from TikTok and YouTube which offer easier content discovery; (4) no sense of community or belonging within the app. His low engagement makes him a low-priority user from a revenue perspective, but his high volume represents significant potential if conversion rates improve.

**Desired Solution:** Simplified match discovery with clear scheduling; short-form highlight content that's easy to consume; trending content indicators that show what the community is watching.

## PART B: PROBLEM DEFINITION & HYPOTHESES

### B1. CORE PAIN POINTS & ROOT CAUSES

FPT Play's Sports Page faces seven critical pain points that collectively prevent the platform from becoming a daily destination:

**Pain Point 1: Single-Session User Behavior (68% of users)**
Current State: 68% of users exhibit "Open → Find match → Watch → Exit" behavior, with 23% 7-day retention (vs. 45% industry average). Users spend only 38 seconds on the homepage before navigating to video content, indicating minimal exploration or discovery.

Root Cause: Absence of personalization and lack of compelling post-match content. Users have no reason to remain in the app after their primary viewing objective is satisfied.

Impact: Massive retention gap (23% vs. 45%) represents the single largest opportunity for improvement. Converting even 10% of single-session users to multi-session users would increase retention to 30%+.

**Pain Point 2: Post-Match Content Underutilization (8% highlight viewership)**
Current State: Only 8% of users watch highlights after matches conclude. Post-match content (highlights, analysis, talkshow) accounts for only 15% of total watch time despite representing significant investment in content creation and licensing.

Root Cause: (1) Lack of intelligent curation—all highlights presented identically regardless of user preferences; (2) No incentive structure to encourage post-match engagement; (3) Highlights not surfaced prominently in the user journey; (4) Absence of short-form content for quick consumption.

Impact: This represents a 5x opportunity—highlight viewership could reach 35-40% with proper personalization and curation. Industry research shows 80%+ of Netflix views come from recommendations, validating the personalization approach.

**Pain Point 3: Information Overload & Poor Content Organization**
Current State: Users report feeling overwhelmed by undifferentiated content. Fans interested in specific sports or teams must scroll through irrelevant content. For example, a badminton enthusiast must navigate through football-dominated feeds to find badminton content.

Root Cause: Flat information architecture with minimal categorization. All users see identical homepage layouts regardless of preferences. Search functionality mixes sports with non-sports content.

Impact: Users abandon the app due to decision paralysis. Niche sport enthusiasts (badminton 35%, tennis 18%) experience particular friction, resulting in below-average engagement for these segments.

**Pain Point 4: Lack of Community & Social Features**
Current State: Users cannot discuss matches, share reactions, or build community within the app. Fans must exit to Facebook groups, Discord, or Telegram to engage with fellow enthusiasts.

Root Cause: No in-app community features, real-time discussion capabilities, or social integration beyond basic sharing.

Impact: Users fragment across multiple platforms, reducing session duration within FPT Play and creating switching risk to competitors offering better community features.

**Pain Point 5: Absence of Habit-Forming Mechanisms**
Current State: Users have no reason to open the app on non-match days. Weekly frequency is only 1.2 sessions/user (vs. 3.5 sessions/week industry benchmark). Users don't develop daily app-checking habits.

Root Cause: No personalized scheduling, no streaks/rewards system, no daily content triggers. App engagement entirely dependent on match schedules rather than user habits.

Impact: Revenue concentration during high-traffic periods (major matches) with significant churn during off-season periods (45% churn after season ends).

**Pain Point 6: Insufficient Personalization & Recommendation**
Current State: All users encounter identical homepage layouts and content recommendations. No team-specific personalization, no content mix optimization based on user preferences, no dynamic content adaptation.

Root Cause: Lack of AI-powered personalization infrastructure. Current system treats all users identically.

Impact: Conversion rate of only 12% (vs. potential 17%+ with personalization). Netflix achieves 80% of views from recommendations—FPT Play's lack of personalization represents significant monetization gap.

**Pain Point 7: Seasonal Revenue Volatility & High Churn**
Current State: 45% churn rate after season ends. Revenue concentration during match seasons with significant drop-off during off-season. User engagement highly dependent on match calendar rather than platform stickiness.

Root Cause: User engagement entirely dependent on live match availability. No mechanisms to retain users during off-season periods.

Impact: Unpredictable revenue, high customer acquisition costs to replace churned users, inability to build stable recurring revenue base.

### B2. BUSINESS OPPORTUNITY & NORTH STAR METRIC

The convergence of these pain points creates a clear business opportunity: **transform the Sports Page from a match-viewing destination into a daily habit**. This transformation directly addresses FPT Play's strategic challenge: building a sustainable, high-engagement sports platform that generates consistent revenue regardless of match schedules.

The opportunity is grounded in three validated market trends: (1) AI-powered personalization is proven to drive engagement (Netflix: 80% of views from recommendations); (2) Post-match content engagement is severely underutilized (8% → 40% potential); (3) Habit-forming mechanics drive retention (gamification + streaks increase retention 20-30%).

**North Star Metric: 7-Day Retention Rate**

We propose **7-day retention** as the primary North Star Metric for this initiative. This metric directly reflects whether the Sports Page has successfully transitioned from a match-viewing destination to a daily habit. The target is to increase 7-day retention from the current 23% to 50% within 6 months.

Rationale for this metric: (1) Retention directly correlates with lifetime value and revenue; (2) 50% retention represents industry-average performance, indicating successful competitive positioning; (3) Retention improvement directly results from addressing core pain points (personalization, post-match content, habit formation); (4) Retention is measurable, trackable, and actionable.

**Secondary Metrics:**
- Session duration: 8 → 25-30 minutes (reflects post-match engagement improvement)
- Weekly frequency: 1.2 → 3.5 sessions/week (reflects habit formation)
- Highlight viewership: 8% → 35-40% (reflects post-match content success)
- Conversion rate: 12% → 17%+ (reflects personalization impact)
- ARPU: $36 → $120+ (reflects premium tier monetization)
- Churn rate: 45% → 25% (reflects seasonal volatility reduction)

### B3. STRATEGIC HYPOTHESES

We propose three core hypotheses about user behavior and market opportunity, each with specific impact predictions:

**Hypothesis 1: Personalization Drives Retention**
"If we personalize the Sports Page around users' favorite teams and sports preferences, 7-day retention will increase from 23% to 40%+ because users will have a reason to check the app daily for team-specific updates."

Rationale: Netflix's 80% recommendation-driven views demonstrate personalization's power. FPT Play's current identical-for-all-users approach wastes this opportunity. Personalizing around favorite teams creates a daily habit trigger ("Check app for Liverpool news").

Expected Impact: +17% retention improvement (23% → 40%), +15 minutes session duration, +4% conversion rate.

Supporting Evidence: Core Enthusiasts (Segment 2) already demonstrate 45-50% retention despite poor personalization—proper personalization could unlock this engagement across all segments.

**Hypothesis 2: Post-Match Content Engagement Unlocks Revenue**
"If we intelligently curate post-match content (highlights, analysis, tactical breakdowns) with AI-powered personalization, highlight viewership will increase from 8% to 35-40% because users will receive content perfectly matched to their preferences and viewing time constraints."

Rationale: Current 8% highlight viewership represents massive underutilization. Research shows 80%+ of Netflix views come from recommendations. Post-match content is "sticky" (users engage longer with learning/analysis content vs. passive viewing). Tactical analysis content commands premium pricing.

Expected Impact: +25% retention improvement (23% → 48%), +12 minutes session duration, +3% conversion rate, +$8 ARPU (premium upsell).

Supporting Evidence: K+ achieves 40-50% retention through strong post-match features. YouTube generates 20-30 minute sessions through recommendation algorithms. FPT Play's post-match content investment (highlights, analysis, talkshow) is currently wasted.

**Hypothesis 3: Habit-Forming Mechanics Reduce Seasonal Churn**
"If we implement habit-forming mechanics (personalized weekly schedules, streaks, rewards, community features), we will reduce post-season churn from 45% to 25% because users will have developed daily app-checking habits independent of match schedules."

Rationale: Current 45% post-season churn indicates users lack motivation to engage during off-season. Habit-forming mechanics (streaks, rewards, predictable rituals) create psychological hooks that persist beyond match schedules. Research shows habits form within 2 months with consistent triggers.

Expected Impact: +20% retention improvement (23% → 43%), +5 minutes session duration, +$200k MRR from reduced churn, 45% → 30% churn rate.

### C2. INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

The redesigned Sports Page implements a **user-centric information architecture** that replaces the current flat, undifferentiated structure with a personalized, hierarchical design:

**Homepage Structure (Personalized Per User):**

1. **Daily Card (Hero Section)**
   - Displays personalized daily content based on user's favorite teams and viewing patterns
   - Examples: "Liverpool plays at 19:00 today—watch these 3 pre-match insights," or "No matches today—here are 2 highlights + 1 tactical analysis matching your interests"
   - Purpose: Immediate value proposition, reduces decision paralysis
   - Addresses: Pain Point 1 (single-session behavior), Pain Point 5 (habit formation)

2. **My Team Universe (Personalized Feed)**
   - Curated content exclusively about user's favorite teams (1-3 teams selected)
   - Sections: Upcoming matches, recent results, highlights, team news, player statistics, community discussions
   - Purpose: Creates "home base" for user's primary interests
   - Addresses: Pain Point 3 (information overload), Pain Point 6 (personalization)

3. **Post-Match Hub (Time-Sensitive)**
   - Appears after matches conclude with curated highlights, analysis, and community reactions
   - AI-curated content based on user preferences (goals-only, tactical, drama, etc.)
   - Purpose: Captures users during high-engagement window after matches
   - Addresses: Pain Point 2 (post-match underutilization), Pain Point 4 (community)

4. **Discover Section (Exploration)**
   - Trending content by sport, recommended content based on viewing history, new content highlights
   - Purpose: Encourages exploration while reducing decision paralysis
   - Addresses: Pain Point 3 (information overload), Pain Point 4 (Curious Samplers segment)

5. **Sport-Specific Feeds**
   - Separate feeds for Football, Badminton, Tennis, Basketball, etc.
   - Each feed curated based on user's engagement with that sport
   - Purpose: Enables niche sport enthusiasts to find content without wading through football
   - Addresses: Pain Point 3 (information overload), Segment 3 (niche enthusiasts)

6. **Community Hub**
   - In-app discussion spaces for teams, sports, and topics
   - Real-time reactions, polls, prediction games
   - Purpose: Replaces external platforms (Facebook, Discord) for fan discussion
   - Addresses: Pain Point 4 (community), Pain Point 7 (engagement)

**Search & Navigation:**
- Sport-specific search (separate from general app search)
- Advanced filtering: by league, team, player, date, match type
- Quick access to favorite teams/leagues (persistent navigation)

This architecture directly addresses the core pain point of information overload by implementing **progressive disclosure**—users see only content relevant to their interests, with easy access to explore beyond their primary interests if desired.

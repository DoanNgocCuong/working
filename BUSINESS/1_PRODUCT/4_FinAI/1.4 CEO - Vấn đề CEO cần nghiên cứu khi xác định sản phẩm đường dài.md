# Vấn đề CEO cần nghiên cứu khi xác định sản phẩm đường dài

## Khác biệt then chốt: PM vs CEO

**PM (Product Manager)** focus vào **tactical execution**: customer problem, MVP scope, feature prioritization, 0→1 product-market fit (timeline: 0-24 tháng).[^1][^2][^3]

**CEO (Chief Executive Officer)** focus vào **strategic direction**: company vision, competitive moat, portfolio of bets, market position, 0→100 company-market fit (timeline: 0-10+ năm).[^4][^5][^1]

***

## 8 Strategic Lenses của CEO khi brainstorm sản phẩm đường dài

### 1. First Principles \& Vision – "Chúng ta tồn tại để làm gì?"

#### Core Questions

**"Fundamental truth nào chúng ta đang build trên đó? Assumption nào chúng ta breaking?"**[^6][^7][^8]

- PM nghĩ: "Users cần tool để research nhanh hơn."
- CEO nghĩ: "Assumption cũ: browsing = general-purpose. Truth: financial workflows khác biệt căn bản. Break: build browser riêng cho finance từ first principles."[^9][^6]

**"Thế giới như thế nào trong 10 năm nếu chúng ta thành công?"**[^10][^11][^1]

- PM: "1000 users trả tiền."
- CEO: "50% financial analysts dùng finAI hàng ngày. Bloomberg Terminal tích hợp finAI agent. Financial decisions 10x nhanh hơn, 100x nhiều thông tin hơn."[^1]

**"Ultimate customer outcome là gì? (Not features)"**[^12][^11][^1]

- PM: "Features: tóm tắt 10-K, alert giá."
- CEO: "Outcome: Financial professionals make better investment decisions, faster, with provable edge."[^12][^1]


#### Why CEO-level

Vision không phải wishful thinking; đây là **strategic anchor** cho mọi quyết định 10 năm tới. PM có thể thay đổi roadmap, nhưng CEO vision phải **stable và inspiring**.[^13][^11][^1]

#### Tools

- **First Principles Thinking (Elon Musk):** Break problem → fundamental truths → rebuild from scratch[^7][^8][^6]
- **Vision Canvas:** Customer outcome + Market state + Company position in 10 years[^10][^1]


#### Output Deliverable

**Product Vision Statement (2-3 câu):**

> "finAI will become the default browser for every financial professional by 2035, rebuilding browsing from first principles around financial intelligence. We enable analysts, advisors, and traders to make 10x faster, 100x better-informed decisions through AI-native workflows purpose-built for finance."

[^11][^1]

***

### 2. Enduring Competitive Moat – "Tại sao competitor không thể copy?"

#### Core Questions

**"Defensible advantage nào chúng ta sẽ có trong Year 5 mà competitor không replicate được?"**[^14][^15][^16]

- PM: "Chúng ta có features tốt."
- CEO: "Moat = (1) Financial workflow data network effect. (2) Compliance-first architecture (hard to retrofit). (3) Domain expertise brand trong finance vertical."[^16][^14]

Warren Buffett: *"A truly great business must have an enduring 'moat' that protects excellent returns on invested capital."*[^17][^14][^16]

**"Làm sao widen moat mỗi năm? Moat-building roadmap?"**[^15][^14]

- PM: "Build more features."
- CEO: "Year 1: Proprietary financial doc parsing data. Year 2: Broker integration moat (exclusive partnerships). Year 3: Portfolio intelligence network effects kick in (10K users = peer insights)."[^14][^15]

**"Nếu Google/Microsoft vào space này ngày mai, họ struggle với cái gì?"**[^15][^14]

- CEO identifies **asymmetric advantages:**
    - Big Tech struggles với: (1) Finance compliance (slow, legal risk-averse). (2) Vertical depth (generic DNA). (3) Trust trong regulated industry (consumer brand ≠ finance brand).[^14][^15]


#### Why CEO-level

Không có moat = high returns attract competition → erode profits. **CEO's \#1 job = build and deepen moat.** PM build features; CEO build **unfair advantages**.[^16][^17][^15][^14]

#### Tools

- **Economic Moat Framework (Buffett):** Network effects, Brand, IP, Switching costs, Cost advantages[^17][^16][^14]
- **Moat Deepening Matrix:** Current strength (1-10) × Investment required × Time to widen[^14]


#### Output

**Moat Strategy Document:**

> "finAI moat = (1) **Financial workflow data network effect:** More users → better portfolio patterns → better AI → more users. (2) **Compliance-first architecture:** Built from day 1 for regulated environment; Big Tech can't retrofit. (3) **Domain brand:** Trusted by finance professionals; consumer AI brands lack credibility in finance."

**Moat Roadmap:**

- Year 1: Proprietary financial document parsing (10K, earnings)
- Year 2: Exclusive broker integrations (switching cost moat)
- Year 3: Network effects threshold (10K users = peer insight value)

[^15][^16][^14]

***

### 3. Portfolio of Strategic Bets – "Balance short-term vs long-term thế nào?"

#### Core Questions

**"Làm sao balance short-term execution với long-term moonshots?"**[^18][^19][^20]

- PM: "Tất cả vào MVP."
- CEO: "McKinsey Portfolio: 70% Core (0-1Y: agentic browser MVP), 20% Adjacent (1-5Y: enterprise platform), 10% Transformational (5Y+: AI-native trading copilot with full automation)."[^19][^18]

**"Bet nào là irreversible (Type 1) vs reversible (Type 2)?"**[^5][^21]

Bezos framework: Type 1 = one-way door (slow, careful). Type 2 = two-way door (fast, iterate).[^5]

- **Type 1 (irreversible):** Vertical focus on finance (pivot = restart company). Compliance-first architecture.
- **Type 2 (reversible):** Which features, pricing model, GTM channels.[^5]

**"3-5 strategic bets cho next 3 years? Bao nhiêu \$ và team per bet?"**[^20][^18]

CEO translates vision into **portfolio of bets** với clear resource allocation:[^18][^20]

- Bet 1: Agentic browser PMF (\$500K, 5 people, 18 months)
- Bet 2: Enterprise tier (\$300K, 3 people, 12 months)
- Bet 3: AI research agent marketplace (\$200K, 2 people, 24 months)


#### Why CEO-level

**Can't be all short-term (no future) or all long-term (no cash).** PM optimizes single product; CEO optimizes **portfolio** across time horizons.[^19][^20][^18]

#### Tools

- **McKinsey Portfolio of Initiatives:** 70-20-10 rule[^18][^19]
- **Bezos Type 1/Type 2 Decision Framework**[^5]
- **Strategic Bet Canvas:** Bet name, Hypothesis, Success metric, Resources, Owner[^20]

[^19][^20][^18]

### 4. Market Position \& Expansion – "Feature, Product, Platform, hay Ecosystem?"

#### Core Questions

**"Chúng ta đang build feature, product, platform, hay ecosystem?"**[^21][^1][^12]

- PM: "Product = agentic browser."
- CEO: "Year 1-2: Product (browser for analysts). Year 3-5: Platform (agent marketplace, data integrations). Year 5-10: Ecosystem (financial OS, developer community, data network)."[^1][^12]

Feature = acqui-hire. Product = exit. Platform = IPO. Ecosystem = multi-generational company.[^1]

**"Adjacent markets nào expand vào sau khi dominate beachhead?"**[^22][^21]

Geoffrey Moore playbook: Beachhead → Adjacent → Total Addressable Market.[^21]

- **Beachhead (Y1-2):** Buy-side analysts
- **Adjacent 1 (Y2-3):** Financial advisors
- **Adjacent 2 (Y3-4):** Corporate dev teams
- **TAM (Y5+):** All knowledge workers doing research

**"Làm sao go từ startup → category king?"**[^21]

Play Bigger framework: Category kings capture 76% of market value.[^21]

- Not "AI browser" (crowded, commodity)
- Not "finance tool" (boring, low margin)
- **NEW CATEGORY: "Financial Intelligence Browser"** – first agentic browser purpose-built for finance.[^21]


#### Why CEO-level

PM thinks in features/releases; CEO thinks in **market evolution**. Wrong positioning = commoditized quickly.[^1][^21]

#### Tools

- **Product Evolution Matrix:** Feature → Product → Platform → Ecosystem[^1]
- **Market Expansion Map (Geoffrey Moore):** Beachhead → Adjacent → TAM[^21]
- **Category Design Framework (Play Bigger book)**[^21]

***

### 5. Business Model \& Unit Economics – "Kiếm tiền thế nào trong 10 năm?"

#### Core Questions

**"Business model Year 1 vs Year 5 vs Year 10?"**[^13][^12]

- PM: "SaaS subscription \$99/mo."
- CEO: "Y1-2: B2B SaaS. Y3-5: Platform (SaaS + marketplace take rate + data licensing). Y5-10: Network (usage-based pricing, enterprise deals, referral economics)."[^13][^12]

**"Ở revenue scale nào moat trở nên unassailable?"**[^16][^14]

CEO identifies **inflection points** where scale = competitive advantage:[^14]

- 1,000 users = proprietary workflow data
- 10,000 users = financial doc parsing accuracy moat (AI trained on unique dataset)
- 50,000 users = network effects (peer portfolio insights)

**"Path đến \$100M ARR? What needs to be true?"**[^22][^12]

CEO reverse-engineers from outcome:[^22]

\$100M ARR = **100K paid users × \$1K ARPU × 80% retention**

Assumptions must hold:

- TAM: 500K analysts × 20% penetration = 100K users
- ARPU expansion: Upsell from \$100 → \$1K average (freemium → pro → enterprise)
- Retention: 80% Net Revenue Retention


#### Why CEO-level

PM optimizes current business model; CEO **evolves business model** as company scales. Different stage = different model.[^12][^13]

***

### 6. Team \& Organization – "Culture \& talent strategy 10 năm?"

#### Core Questions

**"Company culture nào cần để execute 10-year vision?"**[^22][^12][^5]

CEO = Chief Culture Officer. Culture either enables or kills strategy.[^12][^5]

**finAI culture design:**

1. **Finance domain respect:** Hire domain experts, not just tech generalists
2. **AI excellence:** Best ML talent; high bar on model performance
3. **Compliance-first:** No shortcuts; legal review before ship
4. **Speed:** Ship weekly; fast iteration within compliance guardrails

**"Roles nào hire Year 1 vs Year 3 vs Year 5?"**[^5][^22]

Stage-based hiring:[^22][^5]

- **Y0-1:** AI engineer + finance analyst + full-stack dev (generalists + domain experts)
- **Y1-3:** +Head of Product, +Sales lead, +Compliance counsel (specialists + first leaders)
- **Y3-5:** +CTO, +CFO, +CMO (C-suite executives)


#### Why CEO-level

PM hires for team; CEO hires for **company stage evolution**. Wrong hire at wrong stage = culture debt.[^5][^22]

***

### 7. Capital \& Financing – "Fundraising strategy 7 năm?"

#### Core Questions

**"Bootstrap vs raise? If raise, how much and when?"**[^23][^5]

Milestone-based fundraising:[^23][^5]

- **Bootstrap** to \$10K MRR (6 months) – validate PMF
- **\$2M seed** @ \$10M cap (month 6-12) – scale to \$100K MRR
- **\$10M Series A** @ \$50M cap at \$1M ARR (month 24) – build platform
- **\$50M Series B** @ \$200M cap at \$10M ARR (month 48) – scale to category leader

**"Metrics gì must hit để fundable mỗi stage?"**[^23]

2025 fundraising benchmarks:[^23]

- **Seed:** Product + 50 users + \$10K MRR + strong team
- **Series A:** \$1-2M ARR, 100%+ YoY growth, <10% churn, clear moat
- **Series B:** \$10M ARR, 100%+ YoY, expanding margin, platform validated


#### Why CEO-level

Wrong timing/amount can **dilute or kill company**. PM doesn't think about cap table; CEO lives it.[^23][^5]

***

### 8. Risk \& Resilience – "Existential risks \& optionality?"

#### Core Questions

**"3 existential risks có thể kill company? How mitigate?"**[^5][^21]

Pre-Mortem Analysis: Imagine company failed in Year 3. What was cause?[^5]

**finAI existential risks:**

1. **Regulatory:** SEC shuts down for unlicensed investment advice → **Mitigation:** Legal opinion day 1; position as "research tool" not "advisor"
2. **Technical:** AI hallucination destroys trust → **Mitigation:** Human-in-loop; confidence scores; multi-source validation
3. **Competitive:** Bloomberg launches agentic terminal → **Mitigation:** Speed to moat; vertical depth they can't match

**"Làm sao build optionality? Multiple paths to win?"**[^24][^5]

Strategic Options Framework (Real Options Theory):[^24][^5]

- **Plan A:** B2B SaaS to analysts (primary)
- **Plan B:** White-label to wealth management firms (pivot option)
- **Plan C:** Data/API business to Bloomberg/FactSet (monetization option)

Option value = có thể pivot without restarting from zero.[^24][^5]

#### Why CEO-level

Most startups die from **known risks not managed**, not unknown unknowns. CEO's job = identify and monitor tail risks continuously.[^5]

## CEO Strategic Thinking Process: 4-Step Framework

### Step 1: First Principles Breakdown (Week 1-2)

Question everything about existing solutions:[^8][^6][^7]

1. **Identify the problem:** Financial professionals waste 15-20h/week on manual research
2. **Break down assumptions:**
    - Assumption: Browsers must be general-purpose
    - Assumption: AI tools should be horizontal
    - Assumption: Finance workflows = same as consumer workflows
3. **Fundamental truths:**
    - Truth: Financial decisions require provenance, compliance, accuracy
    - Truth: Financial workflows are structurally different (research → analysis → decision → execution)
    - Truth: Trust in finance = existential; one error = game over
4. **Rebuild from truths:** → Browser purpose-built for finance from ground up

[^6][^7][^8]

### Step 2: Vision Articulation (Week 3-4)

**Vision = Customer outcome + Market state + Company position in 10 years:**[^11][^10][^1]

> "By 2035, finAI is the default browser for 50%+ of financial professionals globally. Financial decisions are 10x faster and 100x more informed. Bloomberg Terminal and FactSet have integrated finAI agent modules. We've created a new category: Financial Intelligence Browser."

**Vision characteristics:**[^11][^1]

- **Long-term focus:** 5-10 years
- **Inspirational:** Motivates team and attracts talent
- **Specific enough** to guide strategy, broad enough to allow innovation
- **Stable:** Roadmap changes, vision doesn't

[^11][^1]

### Step 3: Moat Strategy Design (Month 2-3)

**Map competitive advantages across 5 moat types:**[^15][^16][^14]


| Moat Type | finAI Application | Defensibility (1-10) | Time to Build |
| :-- | :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **Network Effects** | Portfolio intelligence network (more users → better insights) | 9 | 3-5 years |
| **Brand** | Trusted by finance professionals for accuracy/compliance | 7 | 2-4 years |
| **Intellectual Property** | Proprietary financial doc parsing models | 6 | 1-3 years |
| **Switching Costs** | Broker integrations, workflow lock-in | 8 | 2-3 years |
| **Cost Advantage** | Vertical focus = lower CAC than horizontal | 6 | 1-2 years |

**Moat-building roadmap:**[^15][^14]

- Year 1: IP moat (proprietary models)
- Year 2: Switching cost moat (broker integrations)
- Year 3-5: Network effects moat (critical mass)

[^16][^14][^15]

### Step 4: Portfolio Allocation (Month 3-6)

**Allocate resources across time horizons:**[^20][^18][^19]


| Horizon | % Resources | Initiatives | Expected ROI Timeline |
| :-- | :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **Core (70%)** | \$700K, 7 people | Agentic browser MVP; PMF with analysts | 0-18 months |
| **Adjacent (20%)** | \$200K, 2 people | Enterprise tier; wealth management platform | 12-36 months |
| **Transformational (10%)** | \$100K, 1 person | AI-native trading copilot (full automation) | 36-60 months |

**Decision framework:**[^18][^5]

- **Type 1 decisions (irreversible):** Vertical focus, compliance architecture → slow, deliberate
- **Type 2 decisions (reversible):** Features, pricing, GTM → fast, iterate

[^19][^20][^18][^5]

## Practical CEO Brainstorming Session Template

### Pre-Work (Solo, 1 week)

1. **First Principles Exercise:**
    - List all assumptions about current solutions
    - Identify fundamental truths
    - Sketch "rebuilt from scratch" vision
2. **Moat Mapping:**
    - Competitive landscape analysis
    - Identify potential moats (network, brand, IP, switching cost, cost advantage)
    - Assess defensibility (1-10 scale)
3. **10-Year Vision Draft:**
    - Customer outcome statement
    - Market state in 2035
    - Company position

### Brainstorming Session (With co-founders/advisors, 4 hours)

**Hour 1: First Principles Challenge**

- Present assumptions
- Debate fundamental truths
- Pressure-test vision

**Hour 2: Moat Strategy**

- Which moats are most defensible?
- What's the moat-building sequence?
- How do we widen moat each year?

**Hour 3: Portfolio of Bets**

- List all potential initiatives
- Categorize: Core (70%), Adjacent (20%), Transformational (10%)
- Allocate resources

**Hour 4: Risk \& Optionality**

- Pre-mortem: What kills us in Year 3?
- Mitigation plan for each risk
- Strategic options (Plan B, Plan C)


### Post-Session Deliverables (1 week)

1. **Vision Document** (1 page)
2. **Moat Strategy** (2 pages)
3. **Portfolio of Bets** (1 page table)
4. **Risk Register** (1 page)
5. **Strategic Roadmap** (1-page visual: Year 1, 3, 5, 10 milestones)

## Lời khuyên cuối cho CEO finAI

### 1. Start with "Why" – First Principles luôn đầu tiên

Đừng bắt đầu với "Chúng ta build AI browser." Bắt đầu với:[^7][^8][^6]

> "Financial professionals waste 15-20h/week on fragmented research because browsers are designed for consumers, not professionals. Finance workflows have unique requirements: provenance, compliance, multi-source synthesis. We rebuild browsing from first principles for this vertical."

### 2. Obsess về Moat từ Day 1

Every feature, every hire, every partnership → ask: **"Does this widen our moat?"**[^14][^15]

Không có moat = làm thuê cho competitors.[^16][^14]

### 3. Portfolio Thinking > Single Bet

Đừng all-in một product. 70-20-10: Core execution + Adjacent bets + Transformational moonshot.[^18][^19]

### 4. Know Your Irreversible Decisions

Vertical focus = Type 1 (one-way door). Tất cả else = Type 2 (two-way door).[^5]

Speed trên Type 2; caution trên Type 1.

### 5. Culture = Strategy's Twin

Nếu culture sai, strategy đúng cũng fail.[^12][^5]

finAI culture = Finance respect + AI excellence + Compliance-first + Speed.

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**finAI Vision (2035):** 30–50% financial professionals dùng finAI làm default research environment; Bloomberg/FactSet tích hợp finAI agent; $100M+ ARR, category leadership "Financial Intelligence Browser".

**Strategic Thesis:** Browsing for finance ≠ browsing for web. Financial workflows cần provenance, compliance, multi-source synthesis. Chúng ta rebuild browsing từ first principles cho vertical này.

**3 Core Moats:**
1. Data network effects (workflow patterns)
2. Compliance-first architecture (hard to retrofit)
3. Vertical brand trust (finance-native)

**Portfolio (70-20-10):**
- 70% Core: Research Agent Extension (0–18 tháng)
- 20% Adjacent: Enterprise tier + Agent Marketplace (12–48 tháng)
- 10% Transform: Trading Copilot + Financial OS (3–10 năm)

**Capital Plan:** Bootstrap → $10K MRR (6 tháng) → Seed $2M (12 tháng) → Series A $10M (24 tháng)

## PART I: FIRST PRINCIPLES & VISION (The WHY)

### 1. First Principles Breakdown

#### Fundamental Truths (không thể bẻ cong)

**Truth #1: Financial workflows ≠ Consumer workflows**
- Consumer web: 1 page = 1 task (search, shop, read)
- Finance research: 1 idea = 10–50 tab (10-K, earnings, news, screener, broker report, comp analysis)
- Current browsing: mở tab → copy-paste → Excel/Notion → tự tổng hợp = **cực kỳ inefficient**

**Truth #2: Accuracy + Provenance = Table stakes**
- Consumer AI: 95% accuracy có thể accept
- Finance: 1 sai số = trust destroyed, deal loss, fiduciary liability
- Mọi kết luận phải: **source + reasoning + confidence score**

**Truth #3: Generic agentic browsers fail finance**
- Comet, Atlas, Dia: optimize cho shopping, travel, general research
- Không hiểu finance semantics (XBRL, ratios, covenants)
- Không integrate broker, portfolio, compliance

#### The Assumption We're Breaking

**Assumption cũ:** "Browsers must be general-purpose. One browser for everything."

**Truth mới:** "Vertical workflows need vertical browsers. Finance browser ≠ general browser."

**Rebuild from scratch:**
- Input layer: Understand financial documents (10-K, earnings, filings)
- Processing: Extract metrics, flag risks, validate multi-source
- Output: 1-page investment snapshot with full provenance
- Compliance: Audit trail, "research tool" not "advisor"

---

### 2. 10-Year Vision

## PART VIII: RISK & RESILIENCE (Pre-Mortem)

### 1. 3 Existential Risks (CEO Pre-Mortem)

**Imagine: finAI failed in Year 3. What was the cause?**

#### Risk #1: Regulatory – "SEC shuts down for investment advice"

**Scenario:** finAI gives investment recommendation (even indirect) → user sues → SEC investigates → company shut down.

**Probability:** Medium (if positioning wrong)  
**Impact:** Game over

**Mitigation:**
- **Legal opinion day 1:** Clear "research tool" vs "investment advisor" positioning
- **No "buy/sell" recommendations:** Only "research snapshot" + "scenario analysis"
- **Disclaimers:** Every output = clear disclaimer
- **Insurance:** Errors & Omissions (E&O) coverage
- **Partnership:** RIA review product positioning

---

#### Risk #2: Technical – "AI hallucination destroys trust"

**Scenario:** finAI extracts wrong number from 10-K (revenue $1M when it's $100M) → user makes bad decision → trust evaporates, brand damaged.

**Probability:** High (hallucination = real problem in financial LLMs)  
**Impact:** High (one incident = churn spike, potential lawsuit)

**Mitigation:**
- **Human-in-loop:** Always show source (quote from document) + confidence score
- **Validation layer:** Compare extracted numbers against multiple sources; flag conflicts
- **Testing:** Extensive validation on real financial documents before launch
- **Transparency:** Show reasoning; let user correct → training signal

---

#### Risk #3: Competitive – "Bloomberg/FactSet launches finAI killer"

**Scenario:** Year 2–3, big tech or incumbents notice finAI traction, launch copycat with unlimited resources.

**Probability:** Very high  
**Impact:** Medium-High (but moat protects if built right)

**Mitigation:**
- **Speed to moat:** Year 1–2, build data moat + compliance advantage fast
- **Vertical depth:** Go so deep in finance that copying = hard (Bloomberg doesn't want to move away from terminal paradigm)
- **Community:** Build developer community around agent marketplace before competitors enter
